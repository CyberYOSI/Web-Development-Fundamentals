Film Archive

Main Collection

1922 · F.W. Murnau · German Expressionism

Nosferatu

Murnau's unauthorized adaptation of Dracula created the visual vocabulary of horror through shadow and light.

1949 · Carol Reed · British Film Noir

The Third Man

Post-war Vienna becomes the backdrop for Greene and Reed's morally complex masterpiece of expressionist noir.

1931 · Fritz Lang · German Expressionism

M

Lang's first sound film masterfully integrated audio with expressionist visuals for psychological terror.

1948 · Vittorio De Sica · Italian Neorealism

Bicycle Thieves

De Sica's humanist masterpiece defined neorealist cinema through location shooting and social consciousness.

1950 · Akira Kurosawa · Japanese Cinema

Rashomon

Kurosawa's narrative innovation explores subjective truth through multiple conflicting perspectives.

1925 · Sergei Eisenstein · Soviet Montage

Battleship Potemkin

Eisenstein's revolutionary montage techniques created visceral political cinema and influenced generations of editors.

1950 · Billy Wilder · American Film Noir

Sunset Boulevard

Wilder's biting Hollywood satire examines fame, delusion, and the cruel passage of time through noir aesthetics.

1960 · Alfred Hitchcock · American Cinema

Psycho

Hitchcock's thriller revolutionized horror cinema through Bernard Herrmann's score and unprecedented narrative shock.

1957 · Sidney Lumet · American Cinema

12 Angry Men

Lumet's tense chamber piece explores prejudice and justice through masterful ensemble acting and spatial tension.
